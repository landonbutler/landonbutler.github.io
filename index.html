Bio

I am a first year Ph.D. student in EECS at Berkeley, advised by Kannan Ramchandran and supported by the NSF GRFP. Previously, I completed my Master's in Data Science and my Bachelor's in Systems Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania, where I researched under the supervision of Alejandro Ribeiro. My research lies at the intersection of network science, machine learning, and game theory, where I design mathematical and computational approaches to model, learn, and infer the dynamics of complex real-world systems. I have applied this work to a diverse set of domains including aviation, social systems, and robotics.
