Hello! I'm Landon, a senior at the University of Pennsylvania. I'm concurrently pursuing a Master's degree in Data Science and a Bachelor's degree in Systems Engineering, seeking to better understand the systems that comprise the world around us from a data-driven perspective.

My research lies at the confluence of network science and machine learning, where I use mathematical and computational approaches to control, learn, and infer the dynamics of complex real-world systems. So far, I've had the opportunity to research for data distribution polices within robot teams, multigraph learning frameworks, and anomaly detection techniques for hypergraphs.

I'm also passionate about sharing about these fields through teaching. At Penn, I've been a teaching assistant for three courses: an undergraduate level course in data science, a master's level course in applied machine learning, and a PhD level course on graph neural networks. You can learn more about my teaching of these courses here!

Outside of academics, I'm a percussionist in the Penn Band and write drum scores in my spare time.
