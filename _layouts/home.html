I am a first year Ph.D. student in EECS at Berkeley, affiliated with BLISS and BAIR. I am advised by Kannan Ramchandran and supported by an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship.

My research lies at the confluence of network science and machine learning, where I use mathematical and computational approaches to control, learn, and infer the dynamics of complex real-world systems. I have applied this work to a diverse set of domains including aviation, social systems, wireless communications, and robotics.

Before Berkeley, I completed my Bachelor's in Systems Engineering and my Master's in Data Science at the University of Pennsylvania. I also had the chance to research under the supervision of Prof. Alejandro Ribeiro and complete my capstone project under Prof. Megan Ryerson.
